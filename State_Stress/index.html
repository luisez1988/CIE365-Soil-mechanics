State of stress

Geotechnical engineers often require to analyses stresses in different angles. E.g: to calculate the bearing capacity of a foundation.

State of stress

2D stresses

plane strain

\(\boldsymbol{\sigma}=\begin{bmatrix} \sigma_{xx}& \tau_{xy} & 0\\ \tau_{yx}& \sigma_{yy} & 0\\ 0& 0 & \sigma_{zz} \end{bmatrix}\)

Plane stress

\(\boldsymbol{\sigma}=\begin{bmatrix} \sigma_{xx}& \tau_{xy} & 0\\ \tau_{yx}& \sigma_{yy} & 0\\ 0& 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix}\)

Axisymmetric

\(\boldsymbol{\sigma}=\begin{bmatrix} \sigma_{xx}& \tau_{xr} & 0\\ \tau_{rx}& \sigma_{rr} & 0\\ 0& 0 & \sigma_{\theta \theta} \end{bmatrix}\)

State of stress in 2D

  • Compressive stresses are positive.
  • Angles are positive counterclockwise from the \(x\) axis.
  • Shear stresses that produce a counterclockwise rotation are positive.

State of stress in 2D

Using equilibrium of forces the following equations are obtained.

The normal stress acting at angle \(\alpha\) is:

\(\sigma_\alpha = \cfrac{\sigma_y+ \sigma_x}{2}+ \cfrac{\sigma_y-\sigma_x}{2} \cos{2 \alpha} + \tau_{xy} \sin 2\alpha\)

Shear stress acting at angle \(\alpha\) is \(\tau_\alpha\)

\(\tau_\alpha= \cfrac{\sigma_y -\sigma_x}{2} \sin 2 \alpha- \tau_{xy} \cos 2\alpha\)

Principal stresses and directions

These are the stresses acting with zero shear stresses in the element faces \(\tau_{xy}=\tau_{xz}=\tau_{yz}=0\)
$$ \boldsymbol{\sigma}= \begin{bmatrix} \sigma_1& 0& 0\\ 0 & \sigma_2 & 0\\ 0 & 0& \sigma_3 \end{bmatrix} $$
  • By convention we name them so that \(\sigma_1 \geq \sigma_2 \geq \sigma_3\).
  • \(\sigma_1\) is the major principal stress and \(\sigma_3\) is the minor principal stress
  • \(\sigma_2\) is the middle stress. In plane strain and stress conditions acts on the direction perpendicular to the plane.

Mohr's circle

The parametric equations for the normal (\(\sigma_{\alpha}\) ) and shear (\(\tau_{\alpha}\)) render a circle in the range of \( 0\leq \alpha \leq 180 \) degrees.
\(\sigma_\alpha= \cfrac{\sigma_1+ \sigma_2}{2} + \cfrac{\sigma_1 - \sigma_3}{2} \cos 2 \alpha\)
\(\tau_\alpha= \cfrac{\sigma_1 - \sigma_3}{2} \sin 2 \alpha\)

Stress invariants

Stress invariants are stress quantities that do not change according to the orientation.
\(\sigma_m\): Is the average compressive stress (i.e., the center of the circle)
\(\sigma_m=\cfrac{\sigma_1+\sigma_3}{2}\)
\(\sigma_d\): Is the difference between the major and minor stress (i.e., the diameter of the circle)
\(\sigma_d=\sigma_1-\sigma_3\)
