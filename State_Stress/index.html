Lateral stress

We have learned how to compute total/effective vertical stresses (normal to the horizontal plane)

  • What about the Horizontal stress?
  • We use the concept of the coefficient of lateral earth pressure
  • When soil is "at rest" (no lateral movement), we call it the at-rest coefficient \(K_0\). $$K_0=\cfrac{\sigma'_h}{\sigma'_v}$$
  • \(\sigma'_h=\) horizontal effective stress
  • \(\sigma'_v=\) vertical effective stress

State of stress

Geotechnical engineers often require to analyse stresses in different angles. E.g: to calculate the bearing capacity of a foundation.

State of stress

2D stresses

Plane strain

\(\boldsymbol{\sigma}=\begin{bmatrix} \sigma_{xx}& \tau_{xy} & 0\\ \tau_{yx}& \sigma_{yy} & 0\\ 0& 0 & \sigma_{zz} \end{bmatrix}\)

Plane stress

\(\boldsymbol{\sigma}=\begin{bmatrix} \sigma_{xx}& \tau_{xy} & 0\\ \tau_{yx}& \sigma_{yy} & 0\\ 0& 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix}\)

Axisymmetric

\(\boldsymbol{\sigma}=\begin{bmatrix} \sigma_{xx}& \tau_{xr} & 0\\ \tau_{rx}& \sigma_{rr} & 0\\ 0& 0 & \sigma_{\theta \theta} \end{bmatrix}\)

State of stress in 2D

  • Normal stress acting at angle \(\alpha\) is \(\sigma_\alpha\)
  • \(\sigma_\alpha = \cfrac{\sigma_y+ \sigma_x}{2}+ \cfrac{\sigma_y-\sigma_x}{2} \cos{2 \alpha} + \tau_{xy} \sin 2\alpha\)
  • Shear stress acting at angle \(\alpha\) is \(\tau_\alpha\)
  • \(\tau_\alpha= \cfrac{\sigma_y -\sigma_x}{2} \sin 2 \alpha- \tau_{xy} \cos 2\alpha\)

Principal stresses and directions

There always exists an angle \(\alpha\) that satisfies \(\tau=0\). Such that: $$ \boldsymbol{\sigma}= \begin{bmatrix} \sigma_1& 0& 0\\ 0 & \sigma_2 & 0\\ 0 & 0& \sigma_3 \end{bmatrix} $$

  • These stresses are the so-called principal stresses. We named them so that \(\sigma_1 \leq \sigma_2 \leq \sigma_3\).
  • \(\sigma_1\) is the major pincipal stress and \(\sigma_3\) is the minor principal stress
  • In plane strain conditions, the stresses acting on the plane of interest are \(\sigma_1\) and \(\sigma_3\). \(\sigma_2\) acts on the direction perpendicular to the plane.

Mohr's circle

For 2D stresses, the state of stress can be represented using Mohr's circle

  • \(\sigma_\alpha= \cfrac{\sigma_1+ \sigma_2}{2} + \cfrac{\sigma_1 - \sigma_3}{2} \cos 2 \alpha\)
  • \(\tau_\alpha= \cfrac{\sigma_1 - \sigma_3}{2} \sin 2 \alpha\)

Stress invariants

Are stresses that do not change depending on the orientation. For example:

  • \(\frac{\sigma_1+\sigma_3}{2}=\) the mean stress. Note that the mean stress that matters is the 3D mean stress \(p=\sigma_1+\sigma_2+\sigma_3 / 3\).
  • \(\sigma_d=\sigma_1-\sigma_3\) the deviator stress. This is what causes failure.

Elastic solution for \(K_0\)

A simplistic, but fundamental model relating stress-strain behavior.

$$\begin{Bmatrix} \varepsilon_x\\ \varepsilon_y\\ \varepsilon_z\\ \gamma_{xy}\\ \gamma_{xz}\\ \gamma_{yz} \end{Bmatrix}=1/E \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -\nu & -\nu & 0 & 0 & 0\\ -\nu & 1 & -\nu & 0 & 0 & 0\\ -\nu & -\nu & 1 & 0 & 0 & 0\\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 2(1+\nu) & 0 & 0\\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & 2(1+\nu)& 0\\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & 2(1+\nu) \end{bmatrix} \begin{Bmatrix} \sigma_x\\ \sigma_y\\ \sigma_z\\ \tau_{xy}\\ \tau_{xz}\\ \tau_{yz} \end{Bmatrix} $$

Using linear elasticity we can find an elastic solution for \(K_0=\nu/(1-\nu)\) (more after shear strength)
