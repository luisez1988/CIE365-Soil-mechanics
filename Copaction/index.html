Why is compaction important?

Civil engineering projects often requires earthwork, movements of earthen materials from one site to another. Compaction is essential to increase the bearing capacity or strength of soils and to significantly reduce the future settlements.

Fabric of granular soils

It denotes the geometric arrangement of particles, particle groups, and pore spaces in a soil.
For coarse particles it is very important
  • Defines how strong the soil is because it configures force chains.
  • It is difficult to measure directly.
  • Soil fabric is unique.
Note: In general the denser the soil, the more contact there is between its particles, and the stronger it is.

Relative density

Relative density

\(D_r\): Measures the packing of coarse soils using a mapping of void ratio compared to its maximum and minimum possible densities. This measurement is a proxy to asses soil fabric of coarse soils.
\(D_r= \cfrac{e_{max}-e}{e_{max}-e_{min}} \times 100\)
  • \(e_{max}\) is the maximum possible void ratio of the soil. ASTM D4253 test explains the procedure to calculate it.
  • \(e_{min}\) is the minimum possible void ratio of the soil. ASTM D4253 explains the procedure to calculate it.

Compaction of loose coarse soils

When \(e=e_{min}\) the soil is at its densest state and \(D_r=100\%\). Densification of coarse soils aims to increase the relative density.
  • For coarse soils, densification is achieved by vibration.
  • Reaching \(D_r=100\%\) is virtually impossible.
  • There are different techniques to densify coarse soils in the field. We will talk about some briefly.
Note: In nature \(D_r=0\%\) is also virtually impossible. Both max and min relative densities would require a high level of organization of millions of particles.

Clay fabric

Electric attraction forces make particles joint to form a floccule. Clay fabric can be flocculated or disperse if floccules are broken apart.
  • Flocculated fabric occurs at low water content.
  • Disperse fabric occurs with large water and mechanical action.
  • Floccules are similar to macroparticles.

Soil compaction

Is a form of soil improvement that consists of increasing its density using dynamic or vibratory loads.

Benefits of soil compaction:

  • Increased soil strength.
  • Lower compressibility.
  • Reduction of water flow.
  • Increased soil stability.
  • Reduction in frost damage.

Proctor test (ASTM D1140, D1557)

The Proctor test's objective is to determine the maximum dry unit weight \(\gamma_{d,max}\) and the optimum water content \(w_{opt}\) for compaction.
  • Soil is pour into a container of known volume in layers.
  • A standard hammer delivers the compaction effort to the soil.
  • The process is repeated with soils having different water contents.

Compaction effort

\(E_{comp}\): Is the total energy per unit of volume delivered to the soil to compact it.
$$E_{comp}=m_h g \cfrac{h_d N_b N_l}{V}$$
  • \(m_h=\) Hammer mass.
  • \(h_d=\) drop height.
  • \(N_b=\) number of blows per layer.
  • \(N_l=\) number of layers.
  • \(V=\) Volume of mold.

Theoretical maximum dry density

We find an expression relating the dry unit weight \(\gamma_{d}\) with the water content \(w\) using phase relationships.

\(\gamma_d=\cfrac{\gamma_w G_s}{1+wG_s/S}\)

\(\gamma_d\) is maximized if \(S=100\%\) (i.e., all air is removed)

It is the line resulting from the theoretical maximum dry density of the soil.
\(\gamma_{d,max}=\cfrac{\gamma_w G_s}{1+wG_s}\)

Results of compaction tests

Results of compaction tests

  • Typically shows a parabola shape.
  • Left to the optimum soil fabric tends to be flocculated.
  • Right to the optimum soil fabric tends to be disperse.
  • \(\uparrow E_{comp}\longrightarrow \uparrow \gamma_d\)
  • \(\uparrow E_{comp}\longrightarrow \downarrow w_{opt}\)
Is the line of all possible combinations of \(E_{comp}\), \(\gamma_{d,max}\), and \(w_{opt}\).

Effect of water content

Is the region of water contents and densities to the left of the line of optimums.
Is the region of water contents and densities to the right of the line of optimums.
  • Compaction on the wet side is more suitable for soils with large expansion potential.
  • Soils compacted in the dry side are more permeable than soils compacted in the dry side.

Compaction specifications

There are two types of compaction specifications: End Product and Method Specifications.
A relative compaction is specified and the contractor finds the means to achieve that goal.
All methods to compact the soil are specified, like a recipe.
\(RC:\) Is the percentage of compaction achieved in the field with respect to a max. dry density obtained in a Proctor test.
\(RC[\%]=\cfrac{\gamma_{d \ \mathrm{field}}}{\gamma_{d, max}} \times 100\)
