Density and unit weight

Ratio of mass (\(m\)) to the volume (\(V\)) it occupies.

$$\rho=m/V$$

Ratio of weight (\(W\)) to the volume (\(V\)) it occupies.

$$\gamma=W/V$$

Density and unit weight

Note that weigh is mass times gravity. Therefore:

\(\gamma=W/V=m g/V= g \rho \)

Thus:

$$\gamma=g \rho$$

Ratio of density of solid (\(\rho\)) to the density of water (\(\rho_w\)).

$$G_s=\rho/\rho_w$$

Volumes are difficult to measure. Typically, only the total volume \(V_t\) can be measured using a control volume technique.

Weights are easy to measure. Initially, the total weight \(W_t\) is measured and then the dry weight is measured using an oven.

From the previous slide we can construct our first relationships:

$$ W_t=W_s+ W_w $$

Similarly for the total volume:

$$ V_t=V_s+V_w+V_a=V_s+V_v $$

With the volume of voids equal to:

$$ V_v=V_w+V_a $$

Measuring water content

Water in soils is measured and reported in different manners. The most common one is the gravimetric water content. But we can also measure the volumetric water content and the degree of saturation.

Is the ratio of weight of water to the weight of solids in the soil. It is often expressed in %.
$$ w[\%]= \frac{W_w}{W_s} \times 100 $$

Example 2.1

A soil sample is put in a container with mass 12.51 gr. The soil plus the container weighs 54.80 gr (mass is measured). The sample was left in the oven overnight at 105\(^o\)C and weighed 50.51 gr. Calculate: (i) the weight of solids, (ii) the weight of water, and (iii) the water content in %.

Is the ratio of the volume of water \(V_w\) to the total volume \(V_t\).

$$ \Theta=\frac{V_w}{V_t} $$

Is the ratio of volume of water \(V_w\) to the volume of voids \(V_v\). It is often reported in % and it is between 0% and 100%.
$$ S[\%]= \frac{V_w}{V_v} \times 100 $$

Measures of void

The most common measures of space in soils are the void ratio and the porosity.

Ratio of volume of voids \(V_v\) to the volume of solids \(V_s\).

$$ e=V_v/V_s $$

Ratio of volume of voids \(V_v\) to the total volume \(V_t\).

$$ n= V_v/V_t $$

Example 2.2

Show that \(n=e/(1+e)\).

Start with definition of porosity

\(n=\frac{V_v}{V_t}\)

Invert the equation:

\(1/n=\frac{V_t}{V_v}= \cfrac{V_s+V_v}{V_v}=1/e + 1\)

Re-invert the equation

\(n=\frac{e}{1+e}\)

Unit weight of soils

Soils' unit weight depends on water content. In addition, we can also calculate the effect of buoyancy on the unit weight.
Is the ratio of the total weight \(W_t\), over the total volume of the soil \(V_t\).
$$ \gamma_t=W_t/V_t $$
Is the unit weight of the soil if the water content was 0.
$$ \gamma_d=W_s/V_t $$
Is the unit weight of soil if the soil was fully saturated.
$$ \gamma_{sat}= W_{sat}/V_t $$
Is also known as the submerged unit weight or the effective unit weigh.
$$ \gamma'=\gamma_{sat}-\gamma_w $$

Example 2.3

Unit weight is the connector between the weights and the volumes in a phase problem. Using phase diagrams find an expression for the total unit weight \(\gamma_t\) and show that \(S e= w G_s\).

Useful relationships

For unit weight:

$$\gamma_t=\cfrac{\gamma_w (G_s+S e)}{1+e} $$

$$\gamma_d=\cfrac{\gamma_w G_s}{1+e} $$

$$\gamma_{sat}=\cfrac{\gamma_w (G_s+ e)}{1+e} $$

$$ \gamma_t=\gamma_d (1+w/100)$$

Relation between saturation, void ratio, specific gravity, and water content:

$$ S e= w G_s $$
