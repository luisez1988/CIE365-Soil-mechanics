Important unit weight relations

In general the density of a material is:. $$\rho=m/V$$ And its unit weight is: $$\gamma=W/V=m g/V= g \rho$$

The specific gravity of a solid is: $$G_s=\rho_s/\rho_w=\gamma_s/\gamma_w$$ With:

  • \(m:\) Mass
  • \(V:\) Volume
  • \(W:\) weight
  • \(g:\) Gravity
  • \(\rho_w=1000\) kg/m\(^3\)
  • \(\gamma_w=9.81\) kN/m\(^3\)

Soil phases

Phase diagram

Void ratio, porosity, and Saturation

  • Void ratio is defined as: $$e=V_v/V_s$$
  • Porosity is defined as: $$n=V_v/V_t$$
  • And saturation: $$S[\%]=\cfrac{V_w}{V_v} \times 100$$
  • \(V_v=\) Volume of voids
  • \(V_w=\) Volume of water
  • \(V_s=\) Volume of solids

Porosity of uniform spherical particles.

Unit weight of soils

  • Total unit weight: $$\gamma_t=W_t/V_t$$
  • Dry unit weight \(S=0\%\) $$\gamma_d=W_s/V_t$$
  • Saturated unit weight \(S=100\%\): $$\gamma_{sat}=W_{sat}/V_t$$
  • Buoyant unit weight: $$\gamma'=\gamma_{sat}-\gamma_w$$

Water content

Gravimetric water content $$w=\cfrac{W_w}{W_s}\times 100$$ We showed that: $$\gamma_t=\gamma_d(1+w/100)$$

Volumetric water content: $$\Theta=\cfrac{V_w}{V_t}$$ If \(S=100\%\): $$\Theta=n$$

Other useful phase relations

For unit weight: $$\gamma_t=\cfrac{\gamma_w(G_s+Se)}{1+e}$$ $$\gamma_d=\cfrac{\gamma_w G_s}{1+e}$$ $$\gamma_{sat}=\cfrac{\gamma_w(G_s+e)}{1+e}$$
