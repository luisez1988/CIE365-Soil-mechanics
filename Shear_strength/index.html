Mohr-Coulomb model

Consider a block sliding on a plane as analogous to a grain of soil sliding on another.
\( \begin{align} f &= W \sin \theta \\ W \cos \theta \tan \phi &= W \sin \theta \\ \tan \phi &= \tan \theta \\ \phi &= \theta \end{align} \)
What are the valid states of stress according to the Mohr-Coulomb model?
What if the block is adhered to the plane?

The cohesion \(c'\) between soil particles adds shear strength to the soil.

\( \tau_f= S= c'+\sigma_n' \tan \phi' \)

The Mohr-Coulomb model is a linearization of the shear strength of soils. This is not necessarily true for all soils.

Soil Dilatancy

Soil dilatancy is the increase in void volume of a soil mass when it is sheared. It has a significant effect on the shear strength of soils.

Effect of water on the shear strength

  • Water can't scape the soil fabric.
  • Shear occurs with \(\Delta V=0\) or zero volume change.
  • Excess pore water pressure is generated.
This is known as the undrained condition.
  • Water drains instantaneously.
  • Volume change during shearing \(\Delta V \neq 0\).
  • Excess pore pressure is not generated.
This is known as the drained condition.
  • If consolidation is faster than shear rate \(\implies\) closer to drained behavior and \(\Delta u \approx 0\).
  • if consolidation is slower than shear rate \(\implies\) akin to undrained behavior and \(\Delta u \neq 0\).
Drained and undrained behavior are idealizations.

Drained vs. undrained in practice

For NC clays on loading stress path short-term condition is critical. At this time the behavior is similar to undrained conditions.
For heavily OC clays on unloading stress path long-term condition is critical. At this time the behavior is similar to drained conditions.

Measuring shear strength of soils

The shear strength of soils is measured in the laboratory or in-situ using different tests. The most common tests are:

Laboratory tests

  1. Direct shear test (DST).
  2. Triaxial test (TX).
  3. Direct simple shear test (DSS).
  4. Ring shear test.
  5. Laboratory vane shear test.

In-situ tests

  1. Standard penetration test (SPT).
  2. Cone penetration test (CPT).
  3. Vane shear test.

Strength parameters of sand

Sand is a permeable material. When loaded, water can drain rapidly and therefore behave in a drained manner. Thus, we often use effective stress parameters.
\(\tau_f=\sigma'_{nf} \tan \phi'\)
Sands have \(c'=0\) unless there is cementation. However, different friction angles exist depending on deformation level or definition of failure.
  • \(\phi'_p=\) peak friction angle
  • \(\phi'_s=\) secant friction angle
  • \(\phi'_r=\) repose friction angle
  • \(\phi'_c=\) critical friction angle

\(K_0\) formula

Analytical and empirical expressions have been developed for \(K_0\) as a function of the friction angle. These equations are often used to determine \(K_0\) for sand

\(K_0^{nc}=1-\sin \phi'_c\) \(\longrightarrow\) For NC soil (Jacky 1944)

\(K_0^{oc}=K_0^{nc} OCR^{0.5}\) \(\longrightarrow\) For OC soil (Meyerhof 1976)

Drained strength of clay

Normally consolidated clay contracts and heavily overconsolidated clay dilates.

N.C. clay

For normally consolidated clay \(c'=0\).

\( \tau_f= \sigma'_{nf} \tan \phi' \)

Heavily O.C. clay

For overconsolidated clay, cohesion is observed. This cohesion is not necessarily due to cementation but due to suction during dilation.

\( \tau_f= c' + \sigma'_{nf} \tan \phi' \)

Undrained strength of clay

When undrained, soils develop excess pore pressure. The result of undrained tests are states of stress shifted to the right and of equal magnitude in the total stress space.

The undrained shear strength \(S_u\) is defined with \(\phi=0\):

\( \tau_f= S_u=c_u \)
The undrained shear strength is related to the consolidation effective stress.
\( \cfrac{S_u}{\sigma'_{vo}}= k \ OCR^b \)

Where:
\(k=\) Constant between 0.23-0.31
\(b \approx 0.8\) Constant
