Process of consolidation

Terzaghi's Consolidation Theory

Results by balancing the mass of water and its volume with the compressibility of the soil skeleton.
\(\cfrac{\partial u}{\partial t}= C_v \cfrac{\partial ^2 u}{\partial z^2}\)
\(C_v= \cfrac{k}{\gamma_w m_v}\)
  • \(u=\) Excess pore water pressure
  • \(t=\) time
  • \(z=\) Direction of flow
  • \(C_v=\) Coefficient of consolidation
  • \(m_v=\) Coefficient of volume change

Drainage length

\(H_{dr}\): Is the length of the shortest path that a water particle will travel to reach a drainage boundary condition.

Normalized time and depth

\(T_v\): Is a dimensionless parameter that maps the time as a function of the coefficient of consolidation and drainage length.
\(T_v= \cfrac{C_v t}{H^2_{dr}}\)
\(Z\): Maps the depth of a compressible soil using the drainage length.
\(Z=z/H_{dr}\)

Analytical solution

It is possible to analytically solve the Terzaghi's 1D consolidation theory. The following assumptions are considered:
  • The soil has uniform compressibility.
  • The initial pore pressure distribution is uniform.
$$\Delta u(z,t)=\Delta \sigma \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \cfrac{4}{(2n+1)\pi}\exp \left[- \cfrac{(2n+1)^2 \pi^2 }{4}T_v\right] \sin \left[\cfrac{(2n+1)\pi}{2}Z\right]$$

Degree of consolidation

We have determined how the pore pressure \(u\) changes with time but not the settlement. Therefore, we need to introduce a smart mapping between the excess pore pressures and the settlements.
\(U\)[%]: Describes the percentage of settlement with respect to the primary consolidation settlement.
\( U(t)=\cfrac{\delta(t)}{\delta_{pc}} \times 100 \)
  • \(t=0 \) \(\longrightarrow\) \(\delta=0\) \(\longrightarrow\) \(U=0\%\)
  • \(t= \infty \) \(\longrightarrow\) \(\delta=\delta_{pc}\) \(\longrightarrow\) \(U=100\%\)

Determining the coefficient of consolidation

  • Need the time-settlement curve
  • Drainage conditions must be known (single vs double drainage)
  • Determine times corresponding to known degrees of consolidation (e.g., \(t_{50}\), \(t_{90}\))

Methods to determine \(C_v\)

  • Casagrande (1940) \(\longrightarrow\) \(t_{50}\)
  • Taylor (1948) \(\longrightarrow\) \(t_{90}\)
  • Robinson and Allam (1996) \(\longrightarrow\) \(t_{22}\)
  • Mesri et al. (1999) \(\longrightarrow\) \(t_{70}\)
  • Feng and Lee (2001) \(\longrightarrow\) \(t_{60}\)

Casagrande's method

\( C_v= 0.197 \cfrac{H_{dr}^2}{t_{50}} \)
Steps to calculate settlement due to P.C. with time:
  1. For a given time \(t\), calculate the corresponding dimensionless time \(T_v\):
    \(T_v=\cfrac{C_v t}{H_{dr}^2}\)
  2. Use any of i) the analytical solution, ii) approximate solutions, iii) tabulated values and obtain the corresponding degree of consolidation \(U(t)\)
  3. Determine the final settlement from primary consolidation \(\delta_{pc}\)
  4. Apply the definition of degree of consolidation and determine the settlement at time \(t\):
    \(U(t)=\cfrac{\delta(t)}{\delta_{pc}}\)

Secondary consolidation

\(\delta_{sc}\): Occurs after all excess pore pressure is dissipated. This is also known as creep.
\( \delta_{sc}= \cfrac{ C_{\alpha} H_o}{1+e_o} \log \left( \cfrac{t_f}{t_{EOP}} \right) \)
  • Is a time dependent slippage between grains, breaking, bending,..
  • Occurs in all type of soils, specially in peats, organic clay, and sensitive clay.

Case history: Kansai International Airport

  • Constructed in the Osaka Bay in 18 m to 20 m depth.
  • Objective was to avoid noise pollution and land acquisition disputes.
  • 2.2 million vertical sand drains used.
  • The island is predicted to settle below sea level by 2067.

Measured and predicted settlements

What problems can arise due to the staggering settlement of the Kansai airport islands?
  • Sea level rise and settlement will interrupt operations.
  • Even if not at SLE yet, the airport becomes more susceptible to weather and natural disasters.
  • This is turn, makes the operation of the airport riskier and more expensive.
  • In the long term, without extraordinary measurements, the airport is doomed to be lost.
What engineering solutions have been implemented so far?
  • Adding fill. However, this exacerbates the settlement problem.
  • Using light-weight materials, but these are usually more expensive.
  • Rising up the terminals.
