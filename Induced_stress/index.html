Induced stresses

Is the increment of stress produced by an external load.
\( \sigma'_f=\underbrace{\color{blue}{\sigma'_0}}_{\substack{\text{In-situ} \\ \text{stress}}}+\underbrace{\color{green}{\Delta \sigma}}_{\substack{\text{Increment of} \\ \text{stress}}}\)
These stresses are absorbed either by the water as excess water pressure or by the soil skeleton as effective stresses. With enough time, the soil skeleton will carry the generated stress.

Infinitely loaded area

When the external load extends considerably in both directions at the surface, it can be modeled as an infinitely loaded area.

The increment of stress for the idealized model is independent of position and depth.

\( \Delta \sigma (x,z)= q \)

Stress distribution due to a point load

Boussinesq's equation for the stress distribution in a elastic semi-infinite medium is:

\( \Delta \sigma_z=\cfrac{3 Q}{2 \pi z^2 \left[1+(r/z)^2\right]^{5/2}} \)

$$\Delta \sigma_z=QI$$

\(I=\) influence factor

Assumptions:
  • Continuous material
  • Isotropic
  • Uniform
  • Linear elastic

Superposition and numerical approximation

  • \(A_i=\) discretized area.
The principle of superposition implies that the total induced stress of multiple loads is the addition of all individual induced stresses.
$$\Delta \sigma_z= \int_{A} I(x,y) q(x,y) dA$$
We can use a numerical approximation instead:
\( \Delta \sigma_z=\sum_{i=1}^n q_i A_i I_i\)

Analytical and chart solutions

Analytical solutions are available for special cases. These solutions are also available in charts and tables.

In general, the solutions are of the form:

\( \Delta \sigma_z= q I' \)

\( I'=\) dimensionless influence factor.
\(q=\) Surcharge load.

Note: You need to take special care to make sure you are using the same:
  • Coordinate system
  • Reference point
These solutions are obtained by analytical integration of the point load equation over defined areas.

Line load

Continuous loading extending infinitely in one direction.
Example:
\( I[1/L]= \cfrac{2 z^3}{\pi (x^2+z^2)^2} \)

Strip load

Cross-sectional load that extends infinitely in one direction.

Uniform load

\( I'[-]= \frac{1}{\pi}[\alpha + \sin \alpha \cos(\alpha + 2 \beta)] \)

Linear load

\( I'[-]=\frac{1}{\pi} \left[ \frac{x}{B} \alpha - \frac{1}{2} \sin (2 \beta)\right] \)
This chart is for the combined action of linear and uniform load at the centerline of the embankment.

Instructions to use charts:

  1. Check definitions in the figure.
  2. Inspect labels in curves and axis.
  3. Determine if problem can be adjusted using the plot definitions.
  4. If yes, calculate normalized values.
  5. Search your curve and find the influence factor.

Circularly loaded area

The following equation is for the stress along the axis of symmetry.
\( I'[-]= 1- \left[\frac{1}{1+ (r_o/z)^2} \right]^{3/2} \)

\(r_o=\) Radius of the loaded area.
\(z=\) Depth of interest.

Rectangular loaded area

The influence factor at the corner of a uniformly loaded rectangular area is:
\( I'[-]= \frac{1}{2\pi} \left[\tan^{-1} \left( \frac{LB}{zR_3} \right)+ \frac{LBz}{R_3} \left( \frac{1}{R_1^2}+\frac{1}{R_2^2}\right) \right] \)

\(L=\) Length of the loaded area.
\(B=\) Width of the loaded area.
\(R_1= \sqrt{L^2+ z^2}\)
\(R_2= \sqrt{B^2 + z^2}\)
\(R_3= \sqrt{B^2+L^2+z^2}\)
