Induced stresses

Is the increment of stress produced by an external load.
\( \sigma'_f=\underbrace{\color{blue}{\sigma'_0}}_{\substack{\text{In-situ} \\ \text{stress}}}+\underbrace{\color{green}{\Delta \sigma}}_{\substack{\text{Increment of} \\ \text{stress}}}\)
These stresses are absorbed either by the water as excess water pressure or by the soil skeleton as effective stresses. With enough time, the soil skeleton will carry the generated stress.

Infinitely loaded area

When the external load extends considerably in both directions at the surface, it can be modeled as an infinitely loaded area.

The increment of stress for the idealized model is independent of position and depth.

\( \Delta \sigma (x,z)= q \)

Boussinesq equation

  • French mathematician and physicist who made contributions to the theory of hydrodynamics, vibration, light, and heat.
  • He derived an equation to find the stress distribution in a semi-infinite elastic medium due to a point load acting on its surface.
  • These equations are closed form solutions derived using continuum mechanics principles and its derivation is not straighfordward.

Stress distribution due to a point load

Boussinesq's equation for the stress distribution in a elastic semi-infinite medium is: $$\Delta \sigma_z=\cfrac{3 Q}{2 \pi z^2 \left[1+(r/z)^2\right]^{5/2}}$$ $$\Delta \sigma_z=QI$$

\(I=\) influence factor

Assumptions:

  • Continous material
  • Isotropic
  • Uniform
  • Linear elastic

Superposition and numerical approximation

  • \(A_i=\) discretized area.
  • The principle of superposition implies that the effect of multiple loads can be taken into account by simple addition.
  • For complex load distributions and shapes use a numerical approximation: $$\Delta \sigma_z=\sum_{i=1}^n q_i A_i I_i$$
