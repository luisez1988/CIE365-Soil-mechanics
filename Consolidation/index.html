Deformation of soils

Recall: Terzaghi's principle \(\longrightarrow\) Whenever there is change in effective stress there is a corresponding deformation.
\(\sigma'=\sigma-u\)
  • Large fill placement \(\longrightarrow\) \(\sigma \uparrow\) \(\longrightarrow\) \(\sigma' \uparrow\) \(\longrightarrow\) settlement
  • Excavation \(\longrightarrow\) \(\sigma \downarrow\) \(\longrightarrow\) \(\sigma' \downarrow\) \(\longrightarrow\) heave
  • Structure load \(\longrightarrow\) \(\sigma \uparrow\) \(\longrightarrow\) \(\sigma' \uparrow\) \(\longrightarrow\) settlement
  • Lowering the water table \(\longrightarrow\) \(u \downarrow\) \(\longrightarrow\) \(\sigma' \uparrow\) \(\longrightarrow\) settlement

Consolidation

Process by which soil's bulk volume decreases due to water scaping from the soil.
Do not confuse consolidation with compaction. Compaction is the process by which soil's bulk volume decreases due to air removal mechanical means.
What is the effect of soil permeability on consolidation?

If we apply a load on...

  • Fully impermeable soil: water cannot leave \(\longrightarrow\) no consolidation \(\longrightarrow\) no settlement.
  • Perfectly permeable soil: Water can leave instantaneously \(\longrightarrow\) instantaneous consolidation\(\longrightarrow\) instantaneous settlement.

Primary consolidation

The settlement due to consolidation in clays can be divided in three different parts:

  • \(S_0=\) Instantaneous settlement
  • \(S_{pc}=\) Primary consolidation
  • \(S_{sc}=\) Secondary consolidation

Primary consolidation process

Oedometer/consolidation test

It is a Laboratory test to determine soil's consolidation properties.

Iterative process:

  1. Increase vertical load \(\Delta \sigma\)
  2. Wait until pore pressure is 100% dissipated (about 24 h).
  3. Measure the settlement \(\Delta S\)
  4. Plot cumulative stress vs settlement.

Settlement vs. void ratio

It is more convenient to plot the consolidation curve in terms of void ration \(e\) than settlement \(S\)

\(\Delta e= \cfrac{\Delta H}{H_0} (1+e_0)\)
  • \(H_0=\) initial height of specimen
  • \(\Delta H=\) settlement (\(S\))
  • \(e_0=\) void ratio
  • \(\Delta e=\) change of void ratio

The consolidation curve

Consolidation parameters

\(C_c\): slope of the virgin compression line in log-space.
\(C_c= -\Delta e/ \log (\sigma'_f/\sigma'_0)\)
\(C_r\): slope of the unloading-reloading line in log-space.
\(C_r= -\Delta e/ \log (\sigma'_f/\sigma'_0)\)

Consolidation parameters

Typical values of \(C_c\) and \(C_r\)

  • \(C_c < 0.05 \) "low" compressibility
  • \(0.05 < C_c < 0.25 \) "medium" compressibility
  • \(C_c>0.25 \) "high" compressibility

Correlation proposed by Terzaghi and Peck (1967) for undisturbed clays of low to medium plasticity.

\(C_c=0.009(LL-10)\)

$$C_r \approx 1/5 -- 1/10 C_c$$

Effect of sample disturbance

We use Casagrande's procedure to correct for disturbance:

  1. Plot consolidation curve form experimental data.
  2. Choose point of maximum curvature.
  3. Draw horizontal line through such point
  4. Bisect the angle between the lines in steps 3 and 4.
  5. Draw virgin compression line.
  6. Intersection of lines in step 5 and 6 is \(\sigma'_c\)

Overconsolidation ratio (OCR)

\(OCR\): Ratio between the preconsolidation stress \(\sigma'_c\) and the in-situ effective stress\(\sigma'_0\)
\(OCR=\cfrac{\sigma'_c}{\sigma'_0}\)
Is a soil with OCR=1. Or a soil in which \(\sigma'_c=\sigma'_0\)
Is a soil with OCR>1. Or a soil in which \(\sigma'_c>\sigma'_0\)
  • Soils (clay) are a history dependent material \(\longrightarrow\) "have memory"
  • The \(OCR\) give us information about the loading geological history of the soil
  • Similarly, if we know some details about the geological history we can make sense of OCR values

Primary consolidation in layered soils

  1. Determine initial vertical effective stresses (\(\sigma'_0\)) at the center of soil layers of concern. If thick layers, divide and find \(\sigma'_0\) for centers of sub-layers.
  2. Extract soil sample at the center of each layer or sub-layer and run consolidation tests.
  3. Determine preconsolidation stress \(\sigma'_c\) and initial void ratio \(e_0=G_s w_0\) for layers of concern.
  4. Determine \(C_c\) and \(C_r\) for layers of concern.
  5. Determine \(\Delta \sigma\) due to external load at the center of each layer of concern.
  6. Compute settlement \(\Delta H\) for each individual layer of concern considering initial high of each layer \(H_0\).
  7. Total settlement \(=\sum \Delta H\)
