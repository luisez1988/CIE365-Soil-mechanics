Deformation of soils

Recall: Terzaghi's principle \(\longrightarrow\) Whenever there is change in effective stress there is a corresponding deformation.
\(\sigma'=\sigma-u\)
  • Large fill placement \(\longrightarrow\) \(\sigma \uparrow\) \(\longrightarrow\) \(\sigma' \uparrow\) \(\longrightarrow\) settlement
  • Excavation \(\longrightarrow\) \(\sigma \downarrow\) \(\longrightarrow\) \(\sigma' \downarrow\) \(\longrightarrow\) heave
  • Structure load \(\longrightarrow\) \(\sigma \uparrow\) \(\longrightarrow\) \(\sigma' \uparrow\) \(\longrightarrow\) settlement
  • Lowering the water table \(\longrightarrow\) \(u \downarrow\) \(\longrightarrow\) \(\sigma' \uparrow\) \(\longrightarrow\) settlement

Consolidation

Process by which soil's bulk volume decreases due to water scaping from the soil.
Do not confuse consolidation with compaction. Compaction is the process by which soil's bulk volume decreases due to air removal mechanical means.
What is the effect of soil permeability on consolidation?

If we apply a load on...

  • Fully impermeable soil: water cannot leave \(\longrightarrow\) no consolidation \(\longrightarrow\) no settlement.
  • Perfectly permeable soil: Water can leave instantaneously \(\longrightarrow\) instantaneous consolidation\(\longrightarrow\) instantaneous settlement.

Total settlement components

The total settlement of a soil can be decomposed into three parts: (i) elastic a.k.a. immediate, (ii) primary consolidation, and (iii) secondary consolidation.
\( \delta_t= \color{green}{\delta_o} + \color{blue}{\delta_{pc}} +\color{red}{\delta_{sc}} \)
\(\color{green}\delta_o=\) Instantaneous settlement.
\(\color{blue}\delta_{pc}=\) Primary consolidation. Time dependent process.
\(\color{red}\delta_{sc}=\) Secondary consolidation. Also known as creep. Is a time-dependent process.

Oedometer/consolidation test

It is a Laboratory test to determine soil's consolidation properties.

Iterative process:

  1. Increase vertical load \(\Delta \sigma\)
  2. Wait until pore pressure is 100% dissipated (about 24 h).
  3. Measure the settlement \(\delta\) or void ratio \(e\).
  4. Plot \(\delta\) or \(e\) vs. time.
  5. Repeat 1-3 for each load increment.
  6. Plot cumulative stress vs settlement or void ratio.
We often use void ration for calculations of settlement. Thus, we want to relate \(\delta\) to \(e\).

Recall: \(\varepsilon_v= \Delta V/ V_o \longrightarrow\) Volumetric strain

For 1D oedometric deformation:
\( \begin{align} \varepsilon_v& = \Delta V/ V_o= \Delta H A /H_o A\\ &= \Delta H / H_o= \varepsilon_a \end{align} \)
Recall \(e=V_v/V_s\)
\( \begin{align} \Delta e& = -\Delta V_v / V_s\\ &=- \Delta H A /[H_o A/(1+e_o)]\\ &=- \Delta H (1+e_o)/H_o \end{align} \)
Two main equations are derived:
\( \varepsilon_a= -\frac{\Delta e}{ 1+ e_o} \)
\( \delta= -\frac{\Delta e H_o}{1+e_o} \)
You will obtain one e vs. time curve for each load increment.
Note: We will look at detail at this curve in the next part of module 6.
You will obtain one consolidation curve per test/sample.
Sample disturbance increases the curvature of the consolidation curve. Casagrande's method suppresses this effect.

Overconsolidation ratio (OCR)

\(OCR\): Ratio between the preconsolidation stress \(\sigma'_c\) and the in-situ vertical effective stress \(\sigma'_{vo}\).
\(OCR=\cfrac{\sigma'_c}{\sigma'_{vo}}\)
Is a soil with OCR=1. Or a soil in which \(\sigma'_c=\sigma'_{vo}\)
Is a soil with OCR>1. Or a soil in which \(\sigma'_c>\sigma'_{vo}\)

Idealized consolidation curve

\(C_c\): slope of the virgin compression line in log-space.
\(C_c= -\Delta e/ \log (\sigma'_f/\sigma'_0)\)
\(C_r\): slope of the unloading-reloading line in log-space.
\(C_r= -\Delta e/ \log (\sigma'_f/\sigma'_0)\)
Note: \(C_r\) and \(C_c\) are soil properties. However, \(\sigma'_c\) and \(OCR\) are state parameters.

Primary consolidation settlement

Settlement in normally consolidated soils
  • Stress path moves along virgin curve
  • Then:
    \( \begin{align} \delta_{pc}&= - \cfrac{H_0 \Delta e}{1+e_0} \\ \end{align} \)
    \(\delta_{pc}= C_c \cfrac{H_0}{1+e_0} \log \left(\cfrac{\sigma'_{vf}}{\sigma'_{vo}}\right)\)

Primary consolidation in layered soils

Follow the steps below to calculate \(\delta_{pc}\) in multilayered soils:
  1. Determine initial vertical effective stresses (\(\sigma'_{vo}\)) at the center of soil layers of concern. If thick layers, divide and find \(\sigma'_{vo}\) for centers of sub-layers.
  2. Extract sample at the center of each layer or sub-layer and run consolidation tests.
  3. Determine \(\sigma'_c\) and initial void ratio \(e_0=G_s w_0\) for layers of concern.
  4. Determine \(C_c\) and \(C_r\) for layers of concern.
  5. Determine \(\Delta \sigma\) due to external load at the center of each layer of concern.
  6. Compute settlement \(\delta_{pc,i}\) for each individual layer of concern considering initial high of each layer \(H_o\).
  7. Total settlement \(=\sum \delta_{pc,i}\)
