Sieve analysis (ASTM D6913)

\(W_i\): Is the weight of soil retained on sieve \(i\).
\(W_{ri}\): Is the cumulative weight retained on sieve \(i\).

\(W_{ri}=\sum_{k=1}^{k=i}W_{k}\)

Note that the total dry weight of the sample is \(W_{td}=\sum_{i=1}^{n_s}W_i\), where \(n_s\) is the total number of sieves used.

Sieve analysis (ASTM D6913)

\(P_{Ri}\): Is the percentage of soil mass retained on sive \(i\).

\(P_{Ri}=\cfrac{W_{ri}}{W_{td}}\times 100\)

\(P_{Pi}\): Is the percentage of soil mass passing sieve \(i\).

\(P_{Pi}=100-P_{Ri}\)

Soil fractions

Soil fractions are the amount of gravel, sand, and fines in a soil.
\(G_F\) : Is the cumulative retained percentage in Sieve No 4 or soil coarser than 4.75 mm.
\(F_F\): Is the portion of soils that passes Sieve No 200 or is finer than 0.075 mm.
\(S_F\): Is the percentage of soil that passes Sieve No 4 and is retained in Sieve No 200.

\(S_F=100-G_F-F_F\)

\(C_F\): Is the portion of soils finer than 2 \(\mu\)m.

Hydrometer analysis

The objective is to determine the GSD of fine grained soils. It complements the sieve analysis and extends the GSD beyond sieve No 200.
  • Segregation of particles by sedimentation (Stokes law).
  • Relative density of suspended flow is measured in time to determine particle diameter.
  • Has several limitations due to unrealistic assumptions.

GSD metrics

Recognizable GSD features:

  • If the GSD is vertical , the soil is uniform , or poorly graded , or well sorted .
  • If the GSD spans across the sizes in a smooth curve the soil is well graded or poorly sorted .
  • If there is a horizontal line in the GSD, the soil is gap graded .

GSD metrics

\(D_x\): Is the particle size for which \(x\) percent of soil is finer thant that diameter.

Common values are:

  1. \(D_{10}\) also known as the effective diameter.
  2. \(D_{30}\).
  3. \(D_{50}\) or median particle size.
  4. \(D_{60}\).

Gradation coefficients:

\(C_u\):

\(C_u=\cfrac{D_{60}}{D_{10}}\)

\(C_c\):

\(C_c=\cfrac{D_{30}^2}{D_{10}D_{60}}\)

Interpolation in the GSD

When dealing with GSD data we will need an interpolation formula to obtain the size corresponding to \(D_x\). Recall that the GSD is plotted in logarithmic space when using interpolation.

Thus a linear interpolation in log space can resolve this issue:

\( \log (D_x/d_i) = \cfrac{\log (d_{i}/d_{i+1}) }{P_{P,i}-P_{P,i+1}} (x-P_{P,i})\)
