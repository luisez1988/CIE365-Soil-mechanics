Sieve analysis (ASTM D6913)

  • Conducted on dry sample. Particles of different diameters are separated by meshes of different appertures.
  • It is performed for coarse soils (gravels and sands \( d>0.075 \) mm or sieve No 200) only.
  • The individual weight retained in sieve \( i \) is measured (\( W_i \)).
  • The percentage retained or coarser than sieve \( i \) is \( R_i=\sum W_i /W_t \).
  • The percentage passing or finner than sieve opening \( i \) is \( P_i=100-R_i \).

Hydrometer analysis

  • Separation of particles by sedimentation (Stacks law).
  • Relative density of suspended flow is measured in time to determine particle diameter.
  • Works for fine soils (\(d<0.075\)mm or sieve No 200).
  • Has several limitations including unrealistic assumptions.

GSD metrics

According to its gradation:

  1. If GSD is vertical soil is uniform or poorly graded or well sorted.
  2. If GSD spans all ranges of soil in a smooth curve the soil is well graded or poorly sorted.
  3. If there is a horizontal line in the GSD the soil is gap graded.

Soil fractions

  • Gravel fraction \(G_F\) is the cummulative retained percentage in Sieve No 4 or \(d>4.75\) mm.
  • Fine fraction \(F_F\) is the portion of soils that passes Sieve No 200.
  • Sand fraction \(S_F=100-G_F-F_F\) is the percentage of soil that passes Sieve No 4 and is retained in sieve No 200 or \(0.075< d \leq 4.75\) mm
  • Clay fraction \(C_F\) is the portion of soils finer than 2 \(\mu\)m.

GSD metrcis

Let's define \(D_x\) as the particle size for which \(x\) percent of teh soil is finer thant that diameter.

Common values are:

  1. \(D_{10}\) also lnown as the effective diameter.
  2. \(D_{30}\).
  3. \(D_{50}\) or median particle size.
  4. \(D_{60}\).

Gradation coefficients:

  • Coefficient of uniformity \(C_u\): $$C_u=\cfrac{D_{60}}{D_{10}}$$
  • Coefficient of curvature \(C_c\): $$C_c=\cfrac{D_{30}^2}{D_{10}D_{60}}$$

Specific surface

Specific surface is defined as: $$SSA= S_a/ M$$ \(S_a\)= Surface area. \(M\)= mass

  • Coarser particles have lower \(SSA\) than fine particles.
  • A high \(SSA\) indicates a higher are for chemical and physical reactions.
  • A high \(SSA\) indicates more area for drag. Finer soils are less permeable than coarse soils.

Clay mineralogy

  • Formed by chemical wheatering of rocks. Form hydrous aluminum silicates also known as phyllosilicates.
  • Only visible with electron microscope and look like plates, flakes, or needles.
  • Only two main blocks: tetrahedral or silica sheets and octahedral or alumina sheets.
  • Different arrangement exists that form the different clay minerals.

Interaction of clay with water

ADD FIGURE
  • Water strongly influences the behavior of fine soils.
  • For minerals with large \(SSA\) and are negatively charged particles.
  • Interaction with water and ions and cations will increase/decrease the diffuse double layer thickness.
  • Changes in the DBL thickness are observed macroscopically as expansion and contraction with changes in moisture content.
  • This interaction with water also creates the plasticity, liquidity, and shrinkage associated with fine soils.

Consistency limits

  • Also known as Atterberg limits upon the Albert Atterberg Agronomer engineer who proposed them.
  • They are thresholds of water content at which the behavior of fine soils changes.
  • After much experimentation he found three practical limits: the liquid limit \(LL\), the plastic limit \(PL\), and the shrinkage limit \(SL\)
